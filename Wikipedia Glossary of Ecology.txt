A branch of ecology which deals with the dynamics of populations within species, and the interactions of these populations with <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Environment (biophysical)"">environmental</a> factors. Also called <i><a href=""#autecology"">autecology</a></i>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_size"" title=""Population size"">population size</a> (Wikipedia Glossary of Ecology)"|"
The number of individuals of a species in a particular population.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Positive_feedback"" title=""Positive feedback"">positive feedback loop</a> (Wikipedia Glossary of Ecology)"|"
A process in which the effects of a small change in a system include an increase in the magnitude of the change; ""A produces more of B, which in turn produces more of A"". Compare <a href=""#negative_feedback_loop"">negative feedback loop</a>.
